What is a Partial Solar Eclipse?

A partial solar eclipse occurs when the Moon partially covers the Sun's disk. Unlike a total solar eclipse, the Sun remains partially visible.

When is the Best Time to View?

The partial eclipse will be visible across various regions, with the peak visibility occurring at different times depending on your location. Check local times for the best viewing opportunities.

Interesting Facts

  • Partial solar eclipses are more common than total solar eclipses.
  • Even during a partial eclipse, it's essential to use proper eye protection to avoid damage.
  • The duration and extent of the eclipse vary by location.
