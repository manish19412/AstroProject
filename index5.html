Orionid Meteor Shower Peak - October 21-22, 2024

What is the Orionid Meteor Shower?

The Orionid meteor shower is an annual event caused by the debris left behind by Halley's Comet. It is known for its fast and bright meteors that radiate from the constellation Orion.

Best Viewing Times

The best time to view the Orionid meteor shower is during the pre-dawn hours. Peak activity will be from October 21 to 22, 2024, with the most meteors visible just before dawn.

Interesting Facts

  • The Orionids are known for their high speed and bright streaks.
  • Halley's Comet is responsible for the Orionid meteors, which are visible every year.
  • Viewing conditions are best under clear, dark skies away from city lights.
