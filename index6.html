Taurid Meteor Shower Peak - November 4-5, 2024

What is the Taurid Meteor Shower?

The Taurid meteor shower is a meteor shower associated with the comet Encke. It's known for its bright fireballs and slow-moving meteors.

Best Viewing Times

Peak visibility occurs late at night and before dawn. The best viewing is away from city lights under a clear, dark sky.

Fun Facts

  • The Taurids are known for their bright, slow-moving meteors.
  • They produce fewer meteors than other showers but often have very bright ones.
  • The shower's activity can last for several weeks, peaking in early November.
