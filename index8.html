Geminid Meteor Shower

Mark your calendars for December 13-14, 2024, as the Geminid Meteor Shower reaches its peak! Known for its bright and multicolored meteors, this shower is a must-see event for stargazers.

The Geminids are unique in that they originate from an asteroid, 3200 Phaethon, rather than a comet. This results in slow-moving, vivid meteors that can produce up to 120 meteors per hour.

For the best viewing experience, find a dark spot away from city lights. The shower is visible all night, but the best time to watch is around 2 AM, when the radiant is highest in the sky.

Prepare for a spectacular night under the stars with a blanket and some hot cocoa. The Geminids are famous for their stunningly bright and often colorful meteors, which can range from yellow to green, blue, or even red.
