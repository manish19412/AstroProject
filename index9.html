Ursid Meteor Shower Peak

December 22, 2024

The Ursid Meteor Shower, occurring annually in late December, is a lesser-known but captivating event for stargazers. Unlike the more famous Geminids, the Ursids offer a serene, almost mystical display of meteors streaking across the night sky.

On the night of December 22, 2024, the Ursid Meteor Shower will reach its peak, providing an excellent opportunity to witness this cosmic event. The Ursids are best viewed from the Northern Hemisphere, where the meteors will appear to radiate from the constellation Ursa Minor.

With a rate of around 5 to 10 meteors per hour, the Ursids are relatively modest compared to other meteor showers. However, their timing near the winter solstice and the lack of moonlight this year will make for dark skies, perfect for meteor watching.

As you prepare for the Ursid Meteor Shower, remember to bundle up, find a spot with minimal light pollution, and allow your eyes to adjust to the darkness. The magic of the Ursids lies in their subtlety, making each meteor sighting a moment of quiet awe.
